Here’s a classic and flavorful Chicken Biryani recipe — rich in spices, aroma, and tradition. It's a layered rice and
meat dish that’s popular across the Indian subcontinent.

🌾 Ingredients

For the Rice:

Basmati rice – 2 cups (soaked for 30 minutes)

Water – 8 cups

Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamom, 1 black cardamom, 4 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick

Salt – 1½ tsp

For the Chicken Marinade:

Chicken – 1 kg (bone-in preferred)

Yogurt – 1 cup

Ginger-garlic paste – 2 tbsp

Red chili powder – 1½ tsp

Turmeric powder – ½ tsp

Garam masala – 1 tsp

Lemon juice – 1 tbsp

Salt – to taste

For the Biryani Base:

Onions – 3 large (thinly sliced)

Tomatoes – 2 medium (chopped)

Green chilies – 2 (slit)

Fresh coriander – a handful (chopped)

Mint leaves – a handful

Biryani masala – 2 tbsp (store-bought or homemade)

Ghee or oil – 5–6 tbsp

Saffron – a few strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

🍳 Instructions

1. Marinate the Chicken (at least 1 hour or overnight):

In a large bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon
juice, and salt. Cover and refrigerate.

2. Cook the Rice:

Bring 8 cups of water to boil with whole spices and salt. Add soaked rice. Boil till 70–80% cooked (the grains
should still have a bite). Drain and set aside.

3. Prepare the Biryani Masala Base:

In a heavy-bottomed pot or large pan, heat oil/ghee.

Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crisp. Remove half for
layering.

Add tomatoes, green chilies, and cook till soft.

Add marinated chicken and cook until it's 80% done.

Add chopped mint and coriander, and biryani masala.

4. Layer the Biryani:

In the same pot or a separate heavy pan, layer:

Half of the chicken masala

Half the rice

Some fried onions, mint, coriander, and a drizzle of saffron milk (if using)

Repeat with remaining chicken and rice.

5. Dum Cooking (Steaming):

Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough) and cook on low heat for 25–30 minutes.

Alternatively, place a tawa (griddle) under the pot to prevent burning.

6. Rest and Serve:

Let it rest for 10 minutes after cooking. Gently fluff with a fork. Serve hot with raita or salan.
